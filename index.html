Akhr in the
Arabic language has
multiple meanings

Akhr in the Arabic language has multiple meanings. Short for alĀkhirah (Arabic: ةرخلآا, derived from Akhir which means last, ultimate, end or close) is an Arabic term for “the Hereafter”.

Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ)

Inspired by the words of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), “The parable of the believers in their affection, mercy, and compassion for each other is that of a body. When any limb aches, the whole body reacts with sleeplessness and fever.”

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) likened the believers to a single body, emphasizing the profound connection they share through their affection, mercy, and compassion for one another. This analogy serves as a guiding principle for harnessing the resource and influence of Muslims in order to create a unified body that collaboratively seeks to achieve common goals. By fostering unity and collective effort, Muslims can harmoniously work towards common objectives, cultivating a spirit of cooperation and empathy that strengthens the entire community. Through this approach, the collective potential and influence of Muslims can be harnessed for the betterment of society as a whole.

Sustaining Generosity: Building a Legacy of
Sadaqah Jariyah and Community Welfare

Discover our mission to create a legacy of lasting charity through Sadaqah Jariyah. We cover burial expenses, facilitate Hajj and Umrah journeys, and invest in sustainable charitable ventures. Join us in building a brighter future for all.

Sadaqah Jariyah

Envision yourself being a part of an ever-growing Sadaqah Jariyah. Our concept revolves around offering Sadaqah Jariyah on behalf of all members past and present, ensuring its perpetuity for future generations until Yawm Al Qiyamah (the Day of Resurrection).

Burial fund

We strive to cover the burial expenses of our members whenever possible, prioritizing our most vulnerable individuals, particularly revert Muslims lacking support or a Muslim family.

Hajj and Umrah fund

Each year, we will endeavor to facilitate the journey of as many individuals as possible to Hajj and Umrah, subject to availability. Sending each person will serve as a sadaqah on behalf of all contributing members

Waqf fund

The Waqf fund has two primary objectives. One is to leverage talent to benefit Muslims whenever possible. The other is to establish profitable business opportunities, with the generated profits being directed towards charitable causes.

Legacy

In accordance with the teachings of the Quran and Sunnah, we embark on Phase 1 of our journey to build a lasting legacy as an ummah. Our aim is to create an enduring impact that can be passed on from generation to generation. To ensure inclusivity, we have designed packages that cater to individuals from all levels of society. By participating in this collective effort we not only benefit in this life, but also in our graves and in the hereafter.
